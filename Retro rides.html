Classic Cars

A selection of the most iconic classic cars in history.

Chevrolet Corvette (1953)

  • Year Launched: 1953
  • Price: $3490
  • America's first sports car, the Corvette became iconic for its fiberglass body and impressive engine performance.

Mercedes-Benz 300SL Gullwing (1954)

  • Year Launched: 1954
  • Price: $6,820
  • Famous for its distinctive gullwing doors, the 300SL combined elegance with groundbreaking engineering.

Porsche 911 (1964)

  • Year Launched: 1964
  • Price: $6,500
  • The original 911 set the foundation for one of the most iconic sports cars in history, blending style, speed, and precision.

Ford Mustang (1965)

  • Year Launched: 1965
  • Price: $2368
  • The Ford Mustang revolutionized the sports car market with its sleek design and powerful performance.

Ford Mustang Eleanor (1967)

  • Year Launched: 1967
  • Price: $7500
  • Made famous by the movie "Gone in 60 Seconds," Eleanor is a customized Shelby Mustang GT500.

Chevrolet Camaro (1967)

  • Year Launched: 1967
  • Price: $2495
  • Designed to compete with the Ford Mustang, the Camaro is known for its performance and style.

Dodge Charger (1969)

  • Year Launched: 1969
  • Price: $3600
  • The 1969 Charger, with its signature split grille, became famous for its role in the TV series "The Dukes of Hazzard."

Ferrari Daytona (1969)

  • Year Launched: 1969
  • Price: $19,500
  • Officially known as the 365 GTB/4, the Daytona was Ferrari's answer to Lamborghini's Miura, blending luxury and performance.

Dodge Challenger (1970)

  • Year Launched: 1970
  • Price: $3100
  • The Challenger was Dodge's entry into the pony car market, offering a variety of powerful engines and aggressive styling.

Plymouth Barracuda (1970)

  • Year Launched: 1970
  • Price: $2846
  • A powerful muscle car, the Barracuda gained fame for its performance and unique design.

Datsun 240Z (1970)

  • Year Launched: 1970
  • Price: $3526
  • A Japanese sports car that combined style, performance, and affordability.

Ford Mustang (1972)

  • Year Launched: 1972
  • Price: $2760
  • The 1972 Mustang retained its bold design but was toned down in power due to stricter emission regulations.
